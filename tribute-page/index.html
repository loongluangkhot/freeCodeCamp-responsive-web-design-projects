Alan Kay

The Father of Object-Oriented Programming

Some facts about Alan Kay:

  • In 1970, Kay joined the Xerox PARC in Palo Alto, California.
  • Using the programming language Smalltalk, Kay developed prototypes of networked workstations.
  • Along with his colleagues, Kay conceptualized the idea of object-oriented programming (OOP), a paradigm that has impacted the field in a very fundamental way.
  • Terms coined by Kay and his colleagues include "object" and "class".
  • According to Kay, the central idea of OOP is about "messaging", rather than "object".
  • Kay was a molecular biology major in college, and his understanding and passion in cellular mechanisms somewhat inspire the birth of OOP.
